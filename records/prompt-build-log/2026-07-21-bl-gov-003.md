## Observed weakness

The old active workflow coupled every atomic evidence step to an issue, branch, full repository validation, software-style version/release cycle, PR, tag, and often a second lifecycle-sync PR. Grandview demonstrated that acquisition, reference inspection, background evidence, proposal, review surface, and response intake can remain independently traceable without each becoming a separately shipped product state. The cadence consumed time and usage while adding little risk reduction between dependent units.

## Bounded improvement

BL-GOV-003 defines three checkpoint classes:

- evidence units retain independent immutable IDs, hashes, gates, dispositions, and failures but may accumulate on a milestone branch;
- milestone checkpoints ship one coherent project-truth change through one issue/branch/PR and only the applicable version/tag/deployment lifecycle;
- exception checkpoints may ship immediately for licensing, security, custody, rollback, stop-condition, or urgent-correctness risk.

Batching changes release cadence only. Source, terms, quality, uncertainty, leakage, owner, rendered-output, privacy, reproducibility, source-precedence, and stop-condition gates remain unchanged.

The prospective Phase Two sequence becomes: six-event evidence milestone under issue #521; dataset-fitness milestone; conditional dataset-and-split milestone; conditional baseline milestone.
