# TERMS-2026-007 - Cross-Event Sentinel Acquisition and Derived Evidence

**Reviewed:** 2026-07-15 UTC

**Issue:** #361

## Decision

`RESOLVED_FOR_BOUNDED_INTERNAL_ACQUISITION_AND_DERIVED_EVIDENCE`.

The current CDSE terms state that Copernicus Sentinel data access and use are free, full, and open subject to the Sentinel Data Legal Notice. Current OData documentation requires an authorized token for product download and identifies the whole-product `$value` route. The owner has authorized the machine-bound CDSE credential. BurnLens may acquire the four exact products into ignored local storage, inspect them, and commit bounded modified evidence with the required source identification, modification notice, no-warranty posture, and no-endorsement boundary.

This checkpoint does not authorize committing or redistributing the native SAFE archives, tokens, cookies, signed routes, provider response bodies containing secret material, or account details. It does not convert MTBS boundaries, SCL classes, catalogue metadata, or derived change measures into ground truth.

## Attribution rule

Derived public evidence must state `Contains modified Copernicus Sentinel data 2017–2018.` and identify the Copernicus programme / European Union source without implying endorsement. MTBS must be attributed to the Monitoring Trends in Burn Severity program jointly administered by USGS and the USDA Forest Service and described as analyst-interpreted reference evidence.
