# TERMS-2026-002 — Exact Asset Route and Retention Review

**Checked:** 2026-07-13

**Decision:** Terms resolved; credential action still requires owner approval

## Copernicus Sentinel-2

The Sentinel Data Legal Notice permits access, reproduction, distribution, communication, adaptation, and modification with attribution. CDSE's current OData documentation requires an authorization token for product download; S3 documentation requires account-generated access/secret keys. General-user quotas apply to OData/S3/STAC traffic and rolling transfer volume.

Future 2024 product use must carry either:

- `Copernicus Sentinel data 2024`, or
- `Contains modified Copernicus Sentinel data 2024`.

The selected OData product can be retained internally as immutable raw research data after an authorized credentialed intake. Redistribution or public bundling of provider bytes is not authorized by this checkpoint and must be reviewed for the exact release package.

## NASA VIIRS

NASA Earthdata's data-use guidance and the exact product pages state that these NASA-led datasets are openly shared without restriction and require proper citation/acknowledgment. Cite both exact products if geolocated active-fire evidence is used:

- `VJ214IMG.002`, DOI `10.5067/VIIRS/VJ214IMG.002`
- `VJ203MODLL.021`, DOI `10.5067/VIIRS/VJ203MODLL.021`

The stable LP DAAC HTTPS routes currently return transient signed redirects without a supplied credential. Signed URLs are delivery details, not durable provenance, and must never be committed. Future internal retention requires local SHA-256 because provider checksums are absent from the current CMR granule metadata.

## Unresolved release questions

No licensing uncertainty blocks internal research acquisition after authorization. Public redistribution of raw or derived source bytes, public screenshots, and final citation text must be re-reviewed against the exact artifact before release. This is a future release decision, not permission to bypass the current credential and source-intake gates.

## P2O1-T03 access clarification — 2026-07-13 local / 2026-07-14 UTC

Fresh review of NASA data-use guidance and both exact product pages confirms that the NASA-led datasets remain openly shared, generally CC0 when no restriction is marked, and should be cited. Separate official Earthdata Login guidance states that DAAC downloads require an Earthdata account and that applications may require authorization. Open-use terms therefore do not imply anonymous byte delivery. This resolves the terms/access distinction but does not authorize adding or using an owner credential.
