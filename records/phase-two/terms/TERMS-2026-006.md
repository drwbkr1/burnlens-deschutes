## Exact-use finding

This checkpoint queries public MTBS, Census TIGERweb, and CDSE STAC metadata; commits a normalized metadata snapshot with bounded MTBS/Census geometries; and records four exact Sentinel-2 product identities for a later acquisition checkpoint. It does not exercise any product download route, retain native provider imagery, create labels, construct a dataset, or redistribute a native Sentinel archive.

U.S. Census TIGER technical guidance states that copyright protection is unavailable for United States government works, permits reproduction of Census materials, and requests Census Bureau source citation. BurnLens cites the Census Bureau and uses GEOID `41017` only to test MTBS representative-point membership.

MTBS is a joint U.S. Geological Survey and USDA Forest Service program. The queried Enterprise Map Service identifies the U.S. Forest Service as service source. USGS copyright guidance states the public-domain posture for USGS-authored works while preserving third-party exceptions. BurnLens attributes both MTBS partners, retains the service copyright text, uses the material as analyst-interpreted cross-fire reference, and makes no endorsement or field-truth claim.

The current CDSE terms state that Sentinel data access and use are free, full, and open subject to the Sentinel Data Legal Notice. The current Sentinel-2 L2A STAC collection declares `license: other` and exposes that legal notice as its exact license link. Any later modified public evidence must preserve the applicable Copernicus source identification, no-warranty posture, and modification notice. Native product redistribution is not authorized by this checkpoint.

## Release boundary
