## Decision

`RESOLVED_FOR_PUBLIC_OFFICIAL_METADATA_AND_BOUNDED_SENTINEL_ACQUISITION_PLANNING; PROVIDER_BYTES_AND_DATASET_NOT_AUTHORIZED_BY_THIS_RECORD`.

The CDSE terms state that access to and use of Copernicus Sentinel data is free, full, and open, governed by the Sentinel Data Legal Notice. The separate portal-content restrictions do not silently replace the Sentinel-data grant. Appropriate credit, modified-data disclosure, no-endorsement language, fair-use behavior, and the service's no-availability/no-suitability posture remain binding.

USGS-authored materials are generally public domain subject to credited third-party exceptions. BurnLens attributes both MTBS partner agencies and preserves exact product notices before any future archive or derived-pixel use. MTBS documentation states that mappings are revised and released periodically, that severity thresholding is analyst-dependent, and that burned-area boundaries are imagery interpretations rather than incident perimeters or field truth.

This checkpoint retains only public metadata and normalized official geometry. It downloads zero provider imagery bytes, redistributes no native archive, creates no derived pixel product, and changes no access or sharing state. Recheck exact archive notices, product terms, and redistribution posture before retaining or publishing any future provider bytes or derivatives.
