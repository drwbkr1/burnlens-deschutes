## Sentinel data rights

The current CDSE terms distinguish Copernicus Sentinel data from other portal material and route Sentinel data use to the European Commission's Sentinel Data Legal Notice. That notice provides free, full, and open access for lawful reproduction, distribution, communication, adaptation, modification, combination, or combinations of those uses, without warranty of quality or fitness.

The exact products in `SOURCE-2026-009` and `SOURCE-2026-010` may therefore be acquired into ignored local research storage and used to produce bounded derived evidence. Any public modified evidence must identify the source with:

`Contains modified Copernicus Sentinel data 2024`.

BurnLens must preserve the no-warranty posture and must not imply Copernicus, the European Commission, ESA, CDSE, or any incident organization endorses, validates, or operates BurnLens.

## Access and quota posture

Current official OData documentation requires an authorization token for product delivery while allowing public catalogue and node listing. Current general-user immediately available data limits state a 12 TB rolling 30-day transfer limit, up to 20 MB/s per connection, and four concurrent connections. The owner reports the quota reset and authorizes the machine-bound CDSE account. The planned approximately 2.255 GB sequential transfer is within those published limits and adds no paid service.

## Product and quality semantics

Official Sentinel-2 Collection 1 L2A documentation describes bottom-of-atmosphere reflectance, JPEG 2000 assets, UTM/WGS84 geometry, SCL and quality indicators, and stated geometric performance. The current PSD 15.0 clarification is material even though these products are baseline 05.10: SCL 2 represents cast shadow, while dark features such as burned areas may be assigned SCL 7 unclassified after baseline 04.00. Neither SCL class may be treated as an automatic burned/background label.
