# TERMS-2026-008 - MTBS Cross-Event Reference Use and Attribution

**Reviewed:** 2026-07-16 UTC

**Issue:** #367

## Decision

`RESOLVED_FOR_BOUNDED_PUBLIC_REFERENCE_ACQUISITION_AND_DERIVED_EVIDENCE`.

USGS states that information it authors or produces is generally public domain, while warning that not everything on a USGS site is USGS-authored and that non-USGS materials may remain protected. The selected service itself credits the Monitoring Trends in Burn Severity Project, U.S. Geological Survey and USDA Forest Service. BurnLens therefore treats these exact service outputs as public federal reference data while preserving the joint program credit and avoiding any claim that the products, agencies, or program endorse BurnLens.

BurnLens may retain the two exact public clips in ignored local storage, transform them with nearest-neighbor categorical resampling for bounded analysis, and commit derived proposal/QA evidence. It may not silently substitute a later service revision, redistribute an asynchronous fire-level bundle without a fresh source/terms review, use third-party site content beyond its terms, or imply that MTBS validates BurnLens labels, accuracy, field condition, operational readiness, or emergency use.

## Required public wording

Derived evidence must identify `Monitoring Trends in Burn Severity (MTBS), U.S. Geological Survey and USDA Forest Service` as the reference source and state that BurnLens modified/resampled the source. The evidence must describe MTBS as analyst-interpreted remotely sensed reference evidence, not field truth or endorsement. Sentinel-derived evidence continues to use the attribution and modification notice in `TERMS-2026-007`.

## Data and access boundary

- Public ImageServer access uses no credential, secret, paid service, account change, or sharing-state change.
- Native MTBS clips remain ignored and untracked; committed outputs are derived BurnLens evidence.
- Exact request, accessed time, service version, byte size, SHA-256, grid, extent, and class domain remain traceable through `SOURCE-2026-013` and the registration manifest.
- Any unresolved future source license, third-party term, or redistribution term is a stop condition.
