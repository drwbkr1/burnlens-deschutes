## Intended future use under review

The prospective route would select an exact current pre/post Sentinel-2 L2A multi-tile roster for later ignored local acquisition, inspection, and attributed modified optical evidence, then separately request and inspect exact Milli BAER/MTBS/RAVG references for program-limited burned, background, unknown/exclusion, and owner-review evidence. Native archives would remain ignored and unpublished. No source would become automatic truth.

U03 decides only whether that route may enter a separate intake contract. It cannot acquire an archive, submit a Portal queue request, instantiate custody, or publish a provider-derived pixel.

## Sentinel-data grant

The current CDSE Terms page continues to distinguish Copernicus Sentinel data from other portal content. The Sentinel Data Legal Notice permits lawful reproduction, distribution, public communication, adaptation, modification, and combination with source identification and no warranty of quality or suitability. The prescribed modified-data identification for a later selected 2017 derivative is `Contains modified Copernicus Sentinel data 2017.` BurnLens may add `accessed through CDSE` as a provenance disclosure, but it does not replace the prescribed wording.

Separate CDSE portal graphics, prose, screenshots, and other content are not silently covered by the Sentinel-data grant. U03 publishes none of them.

The web page reader used for the current semantic check did not expose evidence-grade request timestamps, redirects, bounds, byte counts, or hashes. Exact 2026-07-21 bytes remain preserved in `TERMS-2026-024` and `TERMS-2026-026`, but those records authorize different exact Sentinel products. U03 therefore treats the current semantic observation as supporting context, not a new exact-product authorization.

## Exact blocker and next action

Sentinel use is supportable in principle, but the planned route is not Sentinel-only: the preserved Milli evidence role includes exact BAER/MTBS/RAVG references whose archive notices and derived-publication rights are not yet known. Issue #532 requires affirmative rights before selection, and the current terms attempts also fail exact request-trace requirements. Silence and precedent cannot become permission.

The least-invasive next action is U04 comparison, not another request. A later issue may reconsider Milli with a new immutable run only if stronger exact rights evidence exists before selection or the owner resolves the licensing stop. It must rerun current identity, geometry, STAC/OData, terms, and union/seam gates from zero and must retain the U03 trace failure.

This is a bounded defer, not a claim that Milli or the official programs are permanently unusable.
