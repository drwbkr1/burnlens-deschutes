## Rationale

The U.S. Census representative point for La Pine is `43.6868499, -121.4853152`. The Oregon State Fire Marshal reported that the Darlene 3 Fire started around 1 p.m. June 25, 2024, one mile south of La Pine on the east side of Darlene Way. The envelope deliberately extends beyond that approximate description so metadata queries can find intersecting source scenes without importing an unreviewed incident perimeter.

The event window begins one day before the reported ignition and extends through July 6 to expose pre-event, event, and immediate post-event acquisition availability. It is a discovery window, not a claim about fire duration.

## Explicit non-implications

- This polygon is not an official incident perimeter, evacuation area, ownership boundary, exposure zone, or property-level product.
- It is not the final Phase Two modeling AOI.
- It does not prove that every intersecting scene contains usable imagery or an active-fire detection.
- It may be revised only through a versioned record with rationale and downstream-impact review.
