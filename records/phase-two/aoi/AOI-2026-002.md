## Deterministic derivation

1. Validate the immutable NIFC WFIGS feature snapshot by exact SHA-256 and incident identity.
2. Project every reference coordinate from EPSG:4326 to EPSG:32610.
3. Require the local projected extent to match NIFC's independent `outSR=32610` response within 0.10 m. The observed maximum delta is 0.000220 m.
4. Expand the projected reference extent by 2,000 m on every side.
5. Snap every boundary outward to a 1,000 m grid line.
6. Require complete reference containment, Deschutes County containment, and metadata-footprint coverage for the selected Sentinel/VIIRS pair.

The 2 km context buffer is large enough to preserve surrounding image context and future tiling flexibility without turning the first experiment into a broad county product. The 1 km snap makes the boundary easy to reproduce, inspect, tile, and communicate. Neither parameter implies fire spread, hazard, evacuation, exposure, or uncertainty distance.

## Relationship to AOI-2026-001

`AOI-2026-002` supersedes `aoi-darlene3-discovery-v0.1.0` for modeling. The official reference itself reaches east of the discovery-only envelope, so clipping the final AOI to the earlier planning box would discard source evidence.

The final AOI is 48.096% smaller than the discovery envelope by projected bounding-area comparison, but its east edge is 2,883.302 m farther east. `AOI-2026-001` remains immutable historical evidence for the first metadata queries and is not silently rewritten.
