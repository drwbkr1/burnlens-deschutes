## Authentication boundary

CDSE documents that native OData product download requires an authorization token and S3 requires account-generated access and secret keys. No CDSE account, username, password, token, or S3 key was requested or used.

The two stable NASA HTTPS routes currently generate signed redirects without a supplied credential on HEAD requests. That behavior must be rechecked during a future authorized acquisition. Signed targets are ephemeral and must never be provenance identifiers.

## Subsequent evidence

P2O1-T03 / issue #317 performed the required GET recheck. The redirect behavior does not provide unauthenticated source bytes: a default client ended at `401`, and browser-style requests ended at `200` Earthdata Login HTML. `ACCESS-2026-003` supersedes any inference that the stable routes are no-secret byte-delivery routes. This historical HEAD observation remains valid but is not asset-access authorization.

## Failures and corrections

- A first geolocation lookup used collection version `002` and returned zero results. The official DOI/product page shows that the active NOAA-21 companion is `VJ203MODLL.021`; the corrected query returned the exact matching granule.
- CDSE does not support HEAD on the OData product route. Authentication requirements therefore come from the current official OData and S3 documentation, not from interpreting the `405` as an access decision.
