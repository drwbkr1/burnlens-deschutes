# USE_BOUNDARY-2026-001 — Metadata Discovery

## Allowed

- Reproduce the public metadata queries.
- Inspect provider identifiers, timestamps, footprints, collection fields, and query counts.
- Use the result to choose a later exact asset-readiness checkpoint.
- Cite the source records in internal planning and portfolio-method documentation.

## Not authorized

- Download or retain imagery, fire masks, VIIRS granules, geolocation companions, vectors, perimeters, labels, previews, or thumbnails.
- Infer fire presence from swath or scene intersection alone.
- Treat VIIRS detections as exact perimeters, true 10–20 m labels, or ground truth.
- Create labels, masks, splits, baselines, models, metrics, maps, reports, screenshots, demos, or public analytical claims.
- Use BurnLens for evacuation, routing, suppression, tactical, incident-command, property-level, insurance, legal, or regulatory decisions.
- Add an Earthdata credential, email code, map key, token, paid service, or secret without owner approval.

## Interpretation boundary

The checkpoint proves historical metadata availability and a legally usable research direction. It does not prove that the primary active-fire segmentation target is labelable, that any scene is cloud/smoke free over the AOI, that any VIIRS granule contains a Darlene 3 detection, or that a model will be justified.

## Result

Passed for metadata-only discovery. Every asset and derived-output action remains separately gated.
