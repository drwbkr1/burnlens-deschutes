## Freshness finding

The official 2026 release announcement says the entire archive was reprocessed in late November and December 2025 using contemporary calibration, that distribution was moved and consolidated, that projections moved to native WGS84 UTM, and that deprecated map services moved. It also warns that individual-pixel severity products can differ from legacy products.

`SOURCE-2026-013` therefore remains immutable, reproducible provenance for the already-published 2025-era annual ImageServer proposal inputs. It is not current product-fitness evidence for a new label promotion. No historical artifact is silently rewritten or invalidated.

## Acquisition boundary

The current catalog query is public and uses no credential, secret, paid service, account change, or sharing-state change. Product delivery is a separate step. Exact bundle bytes, internal metadata, product grids, masks, class domains, and bundle-specific non-USGS content have not been accepted by this source record.

Before any label promotion, BurnLens must:

1. receive and preserve exact current product-bundle bytes;
2. review bundle-specific attribution, redistribution, and non-USGS content;
3. verify identity, hashes, metadata, CRS, grid, nodata, class domain, and masks;
4. compare cross-program pixels with the optical evidence and reviewer dispositions; and
5. publish a rendered scientific-fitness decision.
