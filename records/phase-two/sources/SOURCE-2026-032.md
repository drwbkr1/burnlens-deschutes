## Prospective source-project and exclusion rules

These rules are fixed before any NWI feature response is requested or inspected:

1. A source-project feature is eligible only when `PROJECT_NAME`, `DATA_SOURCE`, and `EMULSION` are nonempty and are not the case-normalized placeholders `unknown`, `none`, `null`, `<null>`, `n/a`, or `na`; `STATUS` is exactly `Complete`; and `SOURCE_TYPE` is exactly one of `BW`, `CIR`, or `TC`.
2. `IMAGE_YR` must be a positive integer no later than the 2023 ignition year. A 2023 source requires a parseable exact `IMAGE_DATE` before 2023-08-25. When any `IMAGE_DATE` is present, its year must equal `IMAGE_YR`.
3. `IMAGE_SCALE` must be a positive integer no greater than 100,000 and must also appear in the parsed `ALL_SCALES` value. At least one `SUPPMAPINFO` or `FGDC_METADATA` locator must be nonempty and outside the same placeholder set.
4. The complete frozen optical boundary must have full-pixel eligible source-project coverage. The eligible geometry union is eroded inward by the 20 m optical-cell half-diagonal (`sqrt(200)` meters, approximately 14.142136 m) before pixel-center sampling. An any-touch overlap with an ineligible or unknown source-project geometry excludes the affected optical cells and fails complete eligible coverage; overlap is never resolved in favor of the passing record.
5. Mapped wetland/deepwater geometry is excluded with any-touch rasterization and a conservative policy buffer equal to the greater of 20 m and 1 mm at the maximum eligible source scale (`IMAGE_SCALE / 1000`), with a 100 m hard maximum matching the acquired query halo. This is a BurnLens uncertainty policy, not an official positional-accuracy claim.
6. Missing, malformed, placeholder, post-ignition, above-scale, uncovered, ambiguous-overlap, or unsupported values fail closed. No observed response may relax these rules.

Tracked/public evidence may contain only neutral local feature identifiers or hashes, normalized gate results and reason codes, necessary normalized dates/scales, and aggregate coverage/exclusion measurements. Raw provider geometry and verbatim feature-attribute strings, including project names, source descriptions, emulsion, `ALL_SCALES`, comments, and metadata locators, remain ignored and unpublished.
