## Discovery evidence

NASA CMR returned 124 granule metadata records intersecting `AOI-2026-001` in the event window: 41 S-NPP, 41 NOAA-20, and 42 NOAA-21. Four granule records begin after the official report's approximate 20:00 UTC ignition time on June 25. Exact identifiers and query counts are in `METADATA-2026-001.json`.

Intersection means swath coverage only. The metadata query did not inspect the fire mask and therefore does not establish a detection at Darlene 3.

## Allowed BurnLens role

Reference, cue, sampling aid, weak-label candidate, or non-model baseline after later asset access, geolocation, temporal matching, quality review, and explicit unknown/exclude handling.

## Forbidden role

Pixel-perfect ground truth, exact perimeter, genuine 10–20 m label after resampling, official incident information, or evidence of field validation.

## Limitations

The product detects thermal anomalies, not only vegetation fire. Low-confidence pixels, sun glint, clouds, insufficient background, saturation, scan geometry, parallax, omission errors, false alarms, mixed pixels, and null FRP values require review. Actual geolocation requires the matching `VNP03MODLL`, `VJ103MODLL`, or `VJ203MODLL` companion product. Product improvements and reprocessing can change versions and identifiers.

## Access boundary

FIRMS archive and API delivery were not used because they require Earthdata/email authentication or a map key. No NASA asset URL was opened or downloaded. A future issue must approve and verify any credentialed access route.
