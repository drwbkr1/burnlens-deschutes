The exact ignored USGS delivery binds BAER map `10015623`, MTBS map `10021333`, and RAVG map `10016049`. It contains 53 members, including 20 GeoTIFFs, three four-part burn-area shapefiles, three FGDC metadata records, three ISO metadata records, MTBS/RAVG rendered maps, and KMZ context. Every native raster is EPSG:32610 at 30 m. BAER and MTBS use the same 487 by 347 grid; RAVG has the same dimensions and resolution but is offset 30 m east and 30 m north. Categorical comparison therefore uses nearest-neighbor sampling only and claims no resolution gain.

The BAER and MTBS burn-area SHP, SHX, and PRJ bytes are identical; their DBF attributes differ by program. RAVG has the same one-record bounding box and CRS but different SHP/SHX geometry bytes and program-specific attributes. BurnLens preserves all three identities rather than treating them as interchangeable.

## Product roles

- BAER provides Landsat reflectance plus unthresholded continuous dNBR/RdNBR only. The archive contains no BARC4, BARC256, or soil-burn-severity raster. Continuous change is preliminary context, not a threshold or label.
- MTBS provides dNBR6 classes: 0 background/no data, 1 unburned-to-low or rapid recovery, 2 low, 3 moderate, 4 high, 5 increased greenness, and 6 nonprocessing mask. Classes 2-4 are bounded burned-candidate evidence. Class 1 is not affirmative background truth.
- RAVG provides modeled forest-vegetation effects. CBI4 classes are 0 outside, 1 unchanged, 2 low, 3 moderate, 4 high, and 9 unmappable. Classes 2-4 are bounded burned-candidate evidence. Class 1 can include burned or model-limited conditions and is not affirmative background truth. BA4, BA7, and CC5 remain supporting forest-effect context.

The current WFS request snapshot preserves its raw property-name mapping, where `mod_t=500` and `high_t=259`. The delivered MTBS metadata supplies the governing semantic threshold sequence: no-data `-970`, increased greenness `-150`, unburned/low `50`, low/moderate `259`, and moderate/high `500`. BurnLens records this naming/semantic discrepancy and never silently swaps the raw source fields.

Current viewer products are modernized native-UTM outputs. Provider release notes warn that current imagery selection and calibration can differ from legacy service products. BurnLens does not substitute current and legacy pixels or imply exact historical equivalence.
