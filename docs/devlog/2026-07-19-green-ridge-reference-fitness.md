# Devlog - Green Ridge Reference Fitness

The cycle began by reconstructing the verified v0.33 optical report. It again exposed zero Green Ridge official reference pixels as the highest-leverage evidence weakness. BurnLens submitted the exact current viewer contract once, preserved the acceptance receipt privately, found the resulting delivery in read-only Gmail, verified the private HTTPS route without redirects or substitution, and quarantined the exact archive with no overwrite.

The archive was better and narrower than a superficial “all products” assumption. It contains 20 GeoTIFFs across three programs, but no thresholded BAER BARC or soil-burn-severity raster. That absence resolves the restricted-product concern without weakening the BAER caution: its continuous products remain preliminary context only. MTBS contributes analyst-interpreted dNBR6; RAVG contributes forest-calibrated vegetation-effect categories. Their terms permit this bounded derived evidence with acknowledgement.

The real integrated render makes the result legible. Across 44,110 verified optical-boundary pixels, 41,642 are affirmative in both MTBS and RAVG and 43,533 are affirmative in at least one. The 30 m references are sampled to 20 m with nearest neighbor only, and the report states that no resolution gain exists. BAER/MTBS share one grid while RAVG is offset by one 30 m pixel east and north.

The most important negative finding is product-facing: neither MTBS class 1 nor RAVG class 1 is affirmative background truth. BurnLens therefore ships reference fitness without creating candidates or labels. The next cycle targets affirmative background evidence instead of equating “unchanged,” low effect, outside a program footprint, or lack of agreement with unburned truth.
